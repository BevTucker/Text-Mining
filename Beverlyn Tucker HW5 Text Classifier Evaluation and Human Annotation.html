Introduction¶

Today's generation abundance of available data has encouraged many existing statistical methods to apply predictive and prescriptive forecasting. Classification algorithms often require train data to include target labels, often time-consuming to construct. However, crowdsourcing tools like Amazon Mechanical Turk (MTurk) allow the individual(s) to upload their dataset, then pay another to label the rows of the data for a small price. Enables data scientists to focus on essential data acquisition and more on analysis and reporting.

While MTurk has been around since 2005, many research organizations have either not heard of the platform, or not considered utilizing the tool. Requesters can hire multiple workers within the platform or restrict the type of workers based on satisfied credentials. This allows requesters to restrict who can work on their given dataset selectively. Furthermore, the idea of a "small price" is relative to the size of the dataset. However, careful inferencing could potentially reduce the scope and size.

#  empty array to store Cohen-Kappa test results. 
Kappa_results = []

# Loop over each reviewer and calculate the Cohen-Kappa score against each reviewer 

for i in reviewers[cols]:
    reviewer_compare = reviewers
    for j in reviewer_compare: 
        result = cohen_kappa_score(reviewers[i], reviewer_compare[j])
        Kappa_results.append(result)
        
# Build a pandas DataFrame with the results amd index the name to identify the reviewers. 
Kappa_results = pd.DataFrame(np.array(Kappa_results).reshape(-1, 6))
Kappa_results.set_index(pd.Index(cols), inplace=True)
Kappa_results.columns = cols

Kappa_results = Kappa_results.mask(np.triu(np.ones(Kappa_results.shape, dtype=np.bool_)))

Conclusion¶

Amazon Mechanical Turk (MTurk) allow research studies to utilize unlabeled data by crowdsourcing the labeling responsibilities. During this process, any number of workers can be selected for hire. Prioritization can be established by incentivizing the rate of payment per task. Furthermore, overall scores can be improved by incorporating multiple workers and averaging the total scores. Additional benefits of MTurk include selectively choosing workers by any number of criteria, including geography, age, education, or professional status.

Though results across reviewers are moderate, it could be beneficial to have a larger Datasets. And utilize a platform like an amazon mechanical Turk. Data scientists and research groups focus on deriving research findings.

Overall, the MTurk concept is hugely beneficial. After knowing how much time, I have spent tagging each sentence. Utilizing a crowdsourcing platform is not new and currently being used by large and small organizations alike. While data labeling results are generally more accurate when performed by an internal research group, consideration should be based on optimal time management.
